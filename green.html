Join the Green Revolution

Green bins are an essential part of modern waste management systems, allowing households and businesses to separate organic waste for composting instead of sending it to landfills.

By using green bins properly, you're helping to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, create nutrient-rich compost, and build a more sustainable future for generations to come.

About Green Bins

Green bins are specialized containers designed for collecting organic waste that can be composted. They are typically provided by local municipalities as part of their waste diversion programs.

The organic waste collected in green bins is processed at composting facilities where it breaks down into nutrient-rich compost that can be used to enrich soil in agriculture, landscaping, and home gardens.

This sustainable approach to waste management helps divert significant amounts of waste from landfills, where organic materials would otherwise decompose anaerobically and produce methane, a potent greenhouse gas.

How To Use Your Green Bin

1

Line Your Bin

Use compostable bags or newspaper to line your green bin. This helps keep the bin clean and makes disposal easier. Never use plastic bags, even those labeled "biodegradable."

2

Collect Acceptable Items

Add only organic materials that are accepted by your local program. Common acceptable items include fruit and vegetable scraps, coffee grounds, eggshells, yard waste, and paper food containers.

3

Avoid Prohibited Materials

Keep plastic, metal, glass, pet waste, diapers, and treated wood out of your green bin. These materials contaminate the compost and may cause the entire batch to be rejected.

4

Manage Odors

Reduce odors by wrapping food scraps in newspaper, freezing smelly items until collection day, and regularly cleaning your bin with vinegar or baking soda solutions.

5

Place for Collection

Put your green bin out for collection according to your local schedule. Make sure the lid is securely closed to prevent animals from accessing the contents.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why should I use a green bin?

Using a green bin diverts organic waste from landfills, reducing methane emissions and creating valuable compost. This simple action can reduce your household's environmental footprint by up to 30% and contributes to a circular economy where waste becomes a resource.

How do I prevent odors and pests?

To minimize odors and deter pests, you can: 1) Layer food scraps with yard waste or newspaper, 2) Freeze meat and dairy scraps until collection day, 3) Keep your bin in a shaded area, 4) Clean your bin regularly with vinegar or baking soda solution, and 5) Ensure the lid is always securely closed.

What happens to the collected green bin materials?

After collection, green bin materials are transported to composting facilities where they undergo controlled decomposition. Microorganisms break down the organic matter into compost over several weeks or months. The finished compost is then screened to remove contaminants and is used in agriculture, landscaping, and soil remediation projects.

Can I use plastic bags in my green bin?

No, regular plastic bags should never be used in green bins as they contaminate the compost and do not break down. Only use certified compostable bags that meet your local requirements, paper bags, or newspaper to line your bin. Always check your local guidelines as requirements may vary.

What if I live in an apartment or condo?

Many multi-unit buildings have green bin programs. Talk to your property manager or condo board about available options. You can use a small kitchen counter collector for food scraps and empty it into your building's green bin. If your building doesn't offer green bin service, check if your municipality has community compost drop-off locations.
