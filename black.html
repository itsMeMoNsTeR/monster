Responsible Waste Disposal

Black bins are an essential component of modern waste management systems, designed specifically for non-recyclable waste that must go to landfill.

By using black bins correctly, you're helping ensure that waste is properly contained, collected, and disposed of in an environmentally responsible manner while keeping our communities clean and healthy.

About Black Bins

Black bins are designated containers for collecting non-recyclable household waste destined for landfill. They are typically provided by municipalities as part of their comprehensive waste management programs.

While we strive to reduce the amount of waste going to landfills through recycling and composting programs, some items simply cannot be processed through these streams and must be disposed of properly in black bins.

Modern landfills are engineered with multiple protective layers to safely contain waste and minimize environmental impact. Your proper use of black bins ensures waste makes it to these controlled environments rather than polluting our neighborhoods and natural spaces.

How To Use Your Black Bin

1

Bag Your Waste

Securely tie waste in strong plastic bags before placing in your black bin. This prevents odors, keeps the bin cleaner, and makes collection more efficient.

2

Check For Recyclables

Before disposing of items in your black bin, double-check that they cannot be recycled or composted. This helps minimize unnecessary landfill waste.

3

Don't Overfill

Ensure the bin lid can close completely. Overfilled bins can attract pests, create litter, and may not be emptied by collection services.

4

Hazardous Waste Exclusion

Never place hazardous materials like batteries, electronics, chemicals, or medical waste in your black bin. These require special disposal methods.

5

Place For Collection

Put your black bin out for collection according to your local schedule. Position it with handles facing your property and away from obstacles.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do I need to bag waste before putting it in my black bin?

Bagging waste before placing it in your black bin helps prevent odors, deters pests, makes collection more sanitary for waste workers, prevents items from becoming windblown during collection, and keeps your bin cleaner. It also helps waste management facilities handle materials more efficiently at landfill sites.

What happens to the waste in my black bin?

After collection, waste from black bins is transported to engineered landfill facilities. Modern landfills are designed with multiple protective layers to prevent contamination of surrounding soil and groundwater. The waste is compacted to save space and covered with soil to reduce odors and deter pests. Many facilities also capture methane gas produced by decomposing waste and convert it to energy.

Can I put broken glass in my black bin?

Yes, but it must be properly contained for safety. Wrap broken glass securely in newspaper or place it in a rigid container marked "GLASS" before putting it in your black bin. This protects waste collection workers from injuries. For large amounts of glass, check if your community has a special glass recycling or disposal program.

What should I do with hazardous waste?

Hazardous waste should never go in your black bin. This includes items like paint, chemicals, motor oil, batteries, electronics, and fluorescent bulbs. Most communities offer hazardous waste collection events or permanent drop-off facilities. Check with your local waste management department for specific disposal options in your area.

How can I reduce the amount of waste going into my black bin?

Start by ensuring you're using recycling and composting programs fully. Consider purchasing products with less packaging, buying in bulk, using reusable alternatives to disposable items, donating usable goods instead of discarding them, and repairing items when possible instead of replacing them. Many communities offer waste reduction workshops and resources to help residents minimize their landfill waste.
